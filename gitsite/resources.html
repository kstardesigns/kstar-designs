Graphic Information Technology, as a part of the Human Environmental Systems degree program, teaches social and environmental factors as they relate to technology. In a world that depends on the successful implementation of technological systems, it takes fine-tuned scientists, managers, entrepreneurs and technical operators to manage those systems. Students are prepared as experts in their field for a number of careers after graduation. The Human Environmental Systems Undergraduate programs include:

  • Air Traffic Management
  • Air Transportation Management
  • Applied Science
  • Environmental Resource Management
  • Graphic Information Technology
  • Health Systems Management
  • Industrial and Organizational Psychology
  • Product Development & Manufacturing Technology
  • Professional Flight
  • Technological Entrepreneurship and Management
